The pain is only temporary

I'm not sure where I first heard "the pain is only temporary". It was probably in a David Goggins video or something, not sure.
But what I do know is that pain hurts, (lol) but through pain you find growth.

Personal development and growth isn't a goal but a lifestyle. It is something you do every day. It is the kaizen mindset.

Here are some ways that pain leads to growth:
- pushing sets to failure when working out
- doing the hard work (even when you don't feel like it)
- one more minute
- doing the thing you've been putting off for ages, NOW

Speaking of doing the hard work or the things you've been putting off, I really like the quote:

"The magic you're looking for is in the work you're avoiding." - Dipen Parmar

There is a reason this is true. You are avoiding the work because it is painful, and pain = growth.

The Dipen Parmar quote reminds me of Brian Tracy's idea of "eating the frog". This is where there is a frog (task) in front of you that you don't want to eat (do), but you just need to start eating (working).

Seek out the pain and make it your friend.
